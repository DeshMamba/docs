Chattrvox connects to the tools your team already uses — from CRM platforms and calendar apps to phone carriers and messaging channels. Once an integration is connected, you can use it as a workflow trigger, as a data source in your AI assistants, or as an action node that fires automatically after a call ends. You manage all connections from a single page in your account settings.

## Accessing the integrations page

Navigate to **Settings → Integrations** from the navbar. Each integration card shows its current status — **Available**, **Connected**, or **Configured** — and a **Connect** or **Configure** button.

<Note>
  Only workspace members with the **Manager** role or higher can add or remove integrations. Viewers and members can see which integrations are connected but cannot change them.
</Note>

## CRM integrations

### HubSpot

The HubSpot integration uses OAuth. After you connect, Chattrvox listens for webhook events from your HubSpot portal and can write data back using the HubSpot API. You can use HubSpot contact properties as variables inside any workflow node — for example, `{firstname}`, `{email}`, and `{lifecyclestage}` — whenever the workflow is triggered by a HubSpot event.

See the [HubSpot integration guide](/integrations/hubspot) for full setup instructions.

### GoHighLevel

The GoHighLevel integration also uses OAuth. Connecting a GHL location automatically registers a webhook so that contact creation events flow into Chattrvox in real time. Contact fields from GHL — name, email, phone — are available as variables in downstream workflow nodes.

See the [GoHighLevel integration guide](/integrations/gohighlevel) for full setup instructions.

## Calendar integrations

Chattrvox supports two calendar providers for AI-powered appointment booking:

- **Cal.com** — connect using a Cal.com API key. The event type is selected when you configure the assistant.
- **Google Calendar** — connect using OAuth. The assistant books events directly into your Google Calendar.

When a booking is made during a call, the appointment data is available in post-call workflows through variables like `{appointment.start_time}` and `{appointment.timezone}`.

See the [Calendar integration guide](/integrations/calendar) for full setup instructions.

## Phone and messaging integrations

### Twilio

Twilio provides the underlying phone carrier for Chattrvox. You enter your **Account SID**, **Auth Token**, and **SIP Trunk SID** under **Settings → Integrations**. Once saved, you can assign Twilio numbers to your AI assistants and send SMS messages from workflow **Send SMS** nodes.

<Tip>
  If you are on a managed Chattrvox plan, your platform administrator may have pre-configured Twilio for your workspace. Check with your admin before adding credentials.
</Tip>

### WhatsApp Business

Enter your WhatsApp Business phone number and API key under **Settings → Integrations → WhatsApp**. Connected WhatsApp threads appear in the **Conversations** panel alongside SMS threads.

### SMTP email

Go to **Settings → Integrations → SMTP** and enter your mail server host, port, username, and password. Chattrvox encrypts the password at rest. Once configured, the **Send Email** node is available inside any workflow.

## Automation integrations

### Slack

Click **Connect** on the Slack card and authorize the Chattrvox app in your Slack workspace. After authorization, workflow nodes can send messages to any channel or user your Slack bot can reach.

### Facebook Lead Ads

Click **Connect** on the Facebook Leads card. A two-step OAuth flow first authenticates your Facebook account, then grants page-level permissions so Chattrvox can receive lead form submissions as webhook events. Each new lead can trigger a workflow that calls, texts, or updates your CRM immediately.

<Note>
  The Facebook Lead Ads integration requires your Facebook account to have admin access to at least one Facebook Page with an active Lead Ad form.
</Note>
