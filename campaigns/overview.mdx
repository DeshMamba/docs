Campaigns let you run fully automated outbound dialing at scale. You connect one of your AI assistants to a contact list or CSV import, configure a daily calling schedule, and Chattrvox handles the rest — placing calls, tracking results, and updating contact statuses in real time.

## How a campaign works

When you launch a campaign, Chattrvox queues every contact in your list and begins dialing them during the hours and days you specify. Each call is handled by the AI assistant you selected, using the assistant's configured voice, prompt, and call-analysis settings. After each call, the outcome is recorded and the contact's status is updated automatically.

## Campaign statuses

Every campaign moves through a set of statuses as it progresses.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Idle">
    The campaign has been created and saved but has not been started yet. No calls are being placed.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Running">
    The campaign is active. Chattrvox is dialing contacts according to your schedule and daily cap.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Paused">
    Dialing has been manually paused, or the campaign automatically paused because the daily cap was reached or the current time is outside the configured calling hours. You can resume it at any time.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Completed">
    All contacts have been attempted the maximum number of times allowed. No further calls will be placed.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Error">
    The campaign encountered a problem it could not recover from automatically. Check your assistant's phone number configuration and try restarting.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Note>
  A campaign pauses automatically when it hits the daily cap or when the current time falls outside the configured calling hours. It resumes on the next scheduled calling window.
</Note>

## Retry logic

For simple dial-through campaigns (no workflow attached), you can configure Chattrvox to retry contacts who were not reached. You set the maximum number of attempts per contact, the delay between attempts in minutes, and which outcomes should trigger a retry — for example, no answer, busy, or failed.

<Tip>
  The default retry behavior is one attempt per contact with no retry. Increase max attempts when you want Chattrvox to follow up automatically before marking a contact as complete.
</Tip>

## Workflow campaigns

Instead of a simple dial-through, you can attach a Workflow to a campaign. Workflow campaigns follow a visual automation sequence you build in the Composer — for example: call the contact, wait two hours, send an SMS, then call again. Retry and follow-up logic in workflow campaigns is configured directly inside the workflow using Wait and Call Lead nodes.

## Calling windows

The campaign only dials contacts when all three conditions are met:

- The current day is one of the selected calling days.
- The current hour falls within the configured calling hours window.
- The number of calls placed today is below the daily cap.

If any condition is not met, the campaign pauses and automatically resumes when the next valid window opens.

## Exporting results

You can export all campaign data — call records and summary statistics — to an Excel file from the Campaigns page. Select **Export** from the page header to download a spreadsheet containing every call made across all your campaigns.
